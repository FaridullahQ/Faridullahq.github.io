Cybersecurity starts with strong fundamentals. Before tools, alerts, and investigation workflows, it is important to understand the core ideas that shape defensive security.

## What I am focusing on first

At the start of this journey, the main goal is to understand:

- what cybersecurity really means
- why organizations need it
- the difference between threats, vulnerabilities, and risks
- how the CIA triad shapes security thinking
- why prevention, detection, and response all matter

## Why fundamentals matter

Many beginners move too quickly into tools. That often creates confusion later. A stronger approach is to build the concepts first so every future topic has context.

For example, packet analysis becomes more useful when you already understand confidentiality, integrity, availability, exposure, and attack surface.

## Core concepts to carry forward

### Threat
A threat is anything that can cause harm to systems, users, data, or business operations.

### Vulnerability
A vulnerability is a weakness that can be exploited.

### Risk
Risk is the potential impact when a threat can use a vulnerability successfully.

### CIA triad
- **Confidentiality** protects information from unauthorized access.
- **Integrity** protects information from unauthorized change.
- **Availability** ensures systems and data remain accessible when needed.
