Networking is one of the most important skills in cybersecurity. Without it, alerts, packets, and logs can become difficult to interpret.

## Why networking matters

Security analysts constantly deal with communication between systems. To investigate that communication well, it helps to understand:

- IP addressing
- DNS resolution
- ports and protocols
- HTTP and HTTPS
- packet movement across networks

## Key topics in this stage

### IP addresses
IP addresses identify systems on a network and help explain where traffic comes from and where it is going.

### DNS
DNS translates domain names into IP addresses. Many investigations involve checking whether a domain is trusted, malicious, or unusual.

### Ports and protocols
Ports tell services where to listen. Protocols define how communication happens. This becomes important when reviewing scans, firewall rules, and packet captures.

### HTTP and HTTPS
Web traffic appears everywhere in modern environments. Understanding requests, responses, headers, and encrypted communication is foundational.

## Why this helps a future SOC analyst

Once networking becomes familiar, packet analysis tools like Wireshark and many SIEM alerts make much more sense. Networking is not optional for cybersecurity. It is a core language of the field.
