<section class="page-section">
  <h1>Cybersecurity Labs</h1>
  <p class="section-lead">
    This page tracks the practical side of the journey. Each lab area is selected to support real SOC analyst development and stronger investigation habits.
  </p>

  <div class="labs-grid">
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Network</span>
      <h3>Wireshark Packet Analysis</h3>
      <p>Capture packets, apply filters, inspect protocols, and learn to explain suspicious or unusual traffic clearly.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Recon</span>
      <h3>Nmap Scanning Practice</h3>
      <p>Understand host discovery, port states, service identification, and scan result interpretation.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Host</span>
      <h3>Linux Log Review</h3>
      <p>Use common log files to study authentication events, services, process behavior, and anomalies.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Windows</span>
      <h3>Event Viewer Investigation</h3>
      <p>Review security events, account activity, and signs of suspicious behavior on Windows systems.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Detection</span>
      <h3>SIEM Alert Triage</h3>
      <p>Practice prioritization, context gathering, false positive thinking, and escalation decisions.</p>
    </div>
    <div class="lab-card">
      <span class="lab-tag">Response</span>
      <h3>Incident Workflow Notes</h3>
      <p>Document how alerts move from detection to validation, containment, reporting, and lessons learned.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
</section>

<section class="page-section">
  <h2>How labs will be published</h2>
  <p>
    Each completed lab can later become a post with objective, setup, commands, findings, screenshots, and lessons learned. That structure makes the blog useful both for study and for professional presentation.
  </p>
